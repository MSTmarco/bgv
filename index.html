Today's Approach Doesn't Scale

THE OLD WAY

  • Satellites — expensive, only super-emitters, weather-dependent
  • Drones — non-continuous, weather-dependent, requires certifications
  • Aircraft — expensive, not continuous, heavily regulated
  • Handheld sensors — manual, slow, weather-dependent
  • Fixed sensors — millions to scale and maintain

REACTIVE:

Only confirm leaks while measuring. Cannot determine duration. Miss intermittent leaks entirely.

THE NEW WAY

PREDICTIVE

Use the data you already have. Predict leaks before they happen. Catch intermittent events. 24/7 coverage at a fraction of the cost.

Methane leaks typically last 3–5 hours (Wang et al., 2022; Stokes et al., 2022). To catch every leak with current methods, you'd need continuous sensors everywhere — costing millions.
